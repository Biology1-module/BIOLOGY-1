What I Need to Know

Lesson 8: Repair, Regeneration, and Reproduction

This supplementary module is designed to help you understand how mitosis is essential for cell repair and regeneration and meiosis for reproduction. In this lesson, you will explore how cell division is important in maintaining life’s homeostasis.

Living organisms rely on cell division to maintain life, and two key processes—mitosis and meiosis—play essential roles in repair, regeneration, and reproduction.

Mitosis is responsible for producing genetically identical cells, making it vital for the growth of organisms and the repair of damaged tissue. On the other hand, meiosis is involved in reproduction. Together, mitosis ensures the maintenance and survival of the individual organism, while meiosis supports the continuation of the species through reproduction.

After going through this supplementary module, you are expected to:

  • Describe the role of mitosis in organismal growth, tissue repair, and regeneration of damaged cells.
  • Explain how meiosis contributes to sexual reproduction by producing haploid gametes.
  • Analyze the importance of mitosis and meiosis in maintaining life processes and ensuring genetic continuity and variation.

Answer: Meiosis is essential for reproduction because it produces haploid gametes (sperm and egg) needed for sexual reproduction. It is not used for growth or repair because it creates genetically different cells and reduces chromosome number, which is not suitable for maintaining body tissues.
