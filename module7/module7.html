What I Need to Know

Lesson 7: Meiosis and Crossing Over

This supplementary module is designed to help you understand the phases of meiosis and the importance of crossing over. In this lesson, you will explore the different phases of meiosis and how recombination works in increasing genetic diversity among offspring.

Meiosis is a specialized type of cell division that produces gametes (sex cells such as sperm and egg) with half the number of chromosomes of the original cell. Meiosis occurs in two consecutive divisions: Meiosis I and Meiosis II.

Homologous chromosomes pair up and exchange genetic material through crossing over during prophase I. The outcome of meiosis is four genetically unique haploid cells, each different from the parent cell and from one another. This genetic variation is mainly due to crossing over and the independent assortment of chromosomes. Meiosis plays a crucial role in sexual reproduction, contributing to the diversity seen among organisms.

After going through this supplementary module, you are expected to:

  • Describe the process of crossing over during prophase I of meiosis, including how homologous chromosomes exchange genetic material.
  • Explain how recombination produces new combinations of alleles and contributes to genetic variation in gametes.
  • Analyze the significance of crossing over and recombination in increasing genetic diversity among offspring.

Upon investigation, scientists discover that these differences are linked to genetic variation produced during meiosis, particularly through crossing over and recombination. They now want to understand how this process helped certain individuals survive while others did not.

Answer: Crossing over mixes genetic information by exchanging DNA segments between chromosomes, resulting in new gene combinations in gametes that are different from those of the parents.
Answer: Individuals with more genetic variation are more likely to have traits that help them adapt to environmental changes, resist diseases, and survive challenges.
